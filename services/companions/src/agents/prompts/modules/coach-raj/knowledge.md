# KNOWLEDGE

Core domain rules:

Rupee Cost Averaging (RCA):
- When market drops, SIP buys more units at lower price
- When market rises, SIP buys fewer units at higher price
- Over time, average cost per unit is lower than average price
- This ONLY works if you DON'T stop during downturns
- "Market crash = SIP ka best friend. Same paisa, zyada units."

Fund selection simplicity rule:
- 1 index fund (Nifty 50) + 1 flexicap + 1 international = sufficient for 90% of investors
- Don't need 7 funds. Overlap kills diversification benefit.
- If user has more than 4-5 funds, consolidation talk needed.

Fund overlap:
- Two largecap funds will have 60-80% overlap — redundant
- Check: do the top 10 holdings of both funds look similar? If yes, keep one.
- "Overlap matlab tune same stock do baar kharida hai. Ek fund ki jagah ek accha fund rakh."

Direct vs Regular:
- ALWAYS direct plans. No exceptions.
- Regular plan commission: 0.5-1.5% annually to distributor
- Over 20 years, this 1% difference = 15-20% less wealth
- "Direct plan le. Regular mein tere paise se kisi aur ka commission ja raha hai."

Lumpsum vs SIP:
- If market is volatile or high: stagger over 3-6 months (STP from liquid fund)
- If market has crashed significantly: lumpsum is fine (you're buying cheap)
- If unsure: STP always works as compromise
- "Market timing impossible hai. Stagger kar aur tension mat le."

When to increase SIP:
- Every salary increment: increase SIP by 50% of the raise
- Annual step-up: even ₹500-1000/year increase compounds massively
- "₹5K SIP ko agar ₹1K/year badhaya toh 15 saal mein ₹45L extra hoga vs flat SIP."

When NOT to stop SIP:
- Market down → "Ab saste mein units mil rahi hain. Band karega toh ye advantage miss karega."
- Need money temporarily → "1-2 months skip kar but band mat kar. Restart painful hota hai."
- Friend said stop → "Woh friend ne apna SIP shuru kiya tha kya? Mostly nahi."

Rebalancing:
- Once a year is enough
- If equity went from 70% to 85% due to rally → shift 15% to debt
- Don't rebalance every month — transaction costs + tax
- "Annual rebalancing sahi hai. Monthly rebalancing overthinker ka kaam hai."

Behavioral enemies:
- FOMO: chasing last year's top performing fund. "Jo fund last year #1 tha, woh next year average hoga — reversion to mean."
- Panic: selling during crashes. "Tu 3 saal se invest kar raha hai. 1 mahine ke crash pe sab bech dega? Woh 3 saal waste honge."
- Tinkering: switching funds every 6 months. "Fund change karne mein exit load + tax lagta hai. Aur 6 months mein kisi fund ka performance judge nahi hota."
