# KNOWLEDGE

Core credit score factors (CIBIL/Experian/CRIF):

Payment History (35% weight):
- Most impactful factor
- One 30-day late payment: -50 to -100 points
- 90-day default: severe damage, stays for 3 years minimum
- Recovery: consistent on-time payments for 6-12 months

Credit Utilization (30% weight):
- Outstanding / Credit Limit ratio
- Target: below 30%. Ideal: below 10%.
- Applies per card AND overall
- Trick: pay before statement date to report lower utilization

Credit Age (15% weight):
- Average age of all credit accounts
- Closing your oldest card HURTS this
- Never close old cards even if unused — keep them active with small auto-debit

Hard Enquiries (10% weight):
- Each loan/card application = one hard pull
- 3+ in 60 days = red flag to lenders
- Enquiries drop off in 6-12 months
- Checking your own score = soft pull, no impact

Credit Mix (10% weight):
- Having both secured (home/car loan) and unsecured (credit card) is positive
- Only credit cards = limited. Adding a secured loan helps.
- Don't take a loan JUST for credit mix — not worth the interest

Score ranges:
- 750+: Excellent — best rates, easy approvals
- 700-749: Good — most lenders approve
- 650-699: Fair — higher rates, limited options
- Below 650: Poor — rebuilding needed

The 90-Day Improvement Plan framework:
Every conversation should be able to produce a plan with specific numbered actions, expected timeline for each, projected score impact, and total expected improvement in 90 days.

Example: "Day 1: Pay ₹8K on HDFC card (utilization 45% → 20%, +25-30 points in 45 days). Day 1: Set all EMIs to auto-debit (zero missed payments going forward). Day 30: Do NOT apply for any new credit. Day 90: Expected score 614 → 690-710."

CIBIL Dispute process:
1. Get free annual report from cibil.com
2. Identify wrong entries (wrong accounts, incorrect late payments, identity issues)
3. Raise dispute online — takes 30 days typically
4. If not resolved: escalate to banking ombudsman
5. Unauthorized enquiries: file complaint with the lender who pulled + CIBIL
