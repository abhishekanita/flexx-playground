# KNOWLEDGE

Core domain rules:

HLV (Human Life Value): 10-15x annual income = baseline life insurance cover target.
- ₹8L income → ₹80L-₹1.2Cr life cover minimum
- Add outstanding loans to this number
- Subtract existing cover to find the gap

Term vs Endowment/ULIP:
- ALWAYS recommend pure term insurance + separate investment
- Never recommend bundled insurance-investment products
- Endowment IRR: typically 4-5% (less than PPF)
- ULIP IRR: typically 6-8% after charges (less than direct MF)
- Term insurance cost: ₹500-700/month for ₹1Cr cover at age 25-30

Health insurance essentials:
- Minimum ₹5L cover (₹10L for metro cities)
- Check: no room rent sub-limits, no co-pay, claim settlement ratio 95%+
- Super top-up: ₹15-25L additional cover at very low premium (₹3-5K/year)
- Company health insurance is NOT sufficient — it ends when you leave the job
- Pre-existing disease waiting period: typically 2-4 years. Start early.

Key metrics for comparison:
- Claim Settlement Ratio (CSR): percentage of claims paid. Look for 97%+
- Incurred Claim Ratio (ICR): total claims paid / total premiums collected. 80-100% is healthy.
- Average claim size: indicates whether insurer pays full claims or settles low

Fine print that kills claims:
- Room rent caps: policy says "up to ₹5,000/day room rent" — all treatment costs get proportionally reduced
- Co-pay: you pay 10-20% of every claim. Adds up fast on big bills.
- Sub-limits: "₹50,000 for cataract" when actual cost is ₹80,000
- Waiting periods: 30-day initial, 2-year specific diseases, 4-year pre-existing
- Ambulance cover: usually capped at ₹2,000-5,000

Critical rule: When analyzing existing endowment or ULIP, ALWAYS calculate actual IRR before recommending surrender. Never tell someone to surrender without showing the math.
