# KNOWLEDGE

Core domain rules:

FOIR (Fixed Obligation to Income Ratio) = Total monthly EMIs / Monthly income
- Below 30%: Healthy
- 30-40%: Caution — no new loans without strong justification
- 40-50%: Danger — active reduction needed
- Above 50%: Crisis — load debt-crisis skill immediately

Prepayment math: Always show TOTAL interest saved, not just EMI reduction. Users care about "kitna paisa bachega" not "EMI ₹200 kam hoga."

SIP vs Prepayment framework:
- If loan interest rate > expected SIP CAGR after tax → prepay
- Home loan at 8.5% vs SIP at 12% post-tax ~10% → SIP wins (marginally)
- Personal loan at 14% vs SIP → prepay, always
- The break-even is roughly 10-11%. Above that, prepay.

NBFC vs Bank:
- NBFC rates > 18-20% = predatory territory. Flag immediately.
- Refinancing from NBFC to bank can save 6-10% interest. Always calculate.
- Processing fees for refinancing typically 1-2% — factor into the math.

Home loan rules:
- 20% down payment minimum (lower means higher EMI + PMI equivalent)
- EMI should not exceed 30% of take-home salary
- Total property cost including registration, interiors = 1.3x circle rate
- Floating rate is usually better long-term in India (rates trend down over decades)

Personal loan:
- Interest above 15% = explore alternatives first
- Never for consumptive spending (vacation, wedding beyond means)
- Acceptable for: debt consolidation at lower rate, genuine emergency, business bridge

Education loan:
- Section 80E: full interest deductible, no limit, for 8 years from repayment start
- Moratorium period: interest accrues — pay at least interest during study if possible
- Collateral-free up to ₹7.5L generally
