# Debt Crisis — Situational Skill

For users drowning in debt — multiple EMIs, payday loans, NBFC harassment, or FOIR (Fixed Obligation to Income Ratio) above 50%. This is high-stakes. Wrong advice here causes real harm.

## FOIR Triage

FOIR = Total monthly EMIs / Monthly income

- Below 30%: Manageable. Optimize but not urgent.
- 30-40%: Caution zone. No new loans. Focus on prepayment.
- 40-50%: Danger zone. Active debt reduction needed.
- Above 50%: Crisis. This person cannot take on ANY new debt. Immediate restructuring needed.
- Above 70%: Emergency. Basic expenses are getting squeezed. Needs intervention.

"Teri FOIR 58% hai — matlab ₹53K salary mein se ₹31K sirf EMI mein ja raha hai. ₹22K mein rent, khaana, sab. Ye sustainable nahi hai."

## Debt Repayment Strategies

### Avalanche Method (Mathematically Optimal)
- Pay minimums on everything
- Put all extra money toward the HIGHEST interest rate loan
- Once that's paid off, roll that payment into the next highest rate
- Saves the most money in total interest
- Best for: People who are disciplined and motivated by math

### Snowball Method (Psychologically Optimal)
- Pay minimums on everything
- Put all extra money toward the SMALLEST balance loan
- Quick wins build momentum
- Best for: People who need motivation, who've tried and failed before

"Mathematically avalanche better hai. But agar tujhe motivation chahiye — chhota loan pehle band kar. Woh feeling matters."

## Payday Loan / App Loan Spiral Response

This is the most dangerous debt situation in India right now:

1. DO NOT take a new payday loan to pay an old one — this is exactly how spirals work
2. These apps charge 1-3% PER DAY — that's 365-1095% annualized
3. Many of these apps are ILLEGAL (not registered with RBI)
4. They WILL harass you and your contacts — this is illegal under RBI guidelines

### Action Plan for Payday Loan Victims:
1. List ALL outstanding amounts, interest rates, and app names
2. Stop ALL new borrowing immediately
3. Check if the app is RBI-registered: search on rbi.org.in NBFC list
4. If not registered → file complaint on RBI Sachet portal + cybercrime.gov.in
5. If registered but charging >36% annual → file complaint with RBI ombudsman
6. Prioritize paying off highest-rate app first
7. If harassment starts → screenshot everything, file police complaint under IPC 506 (criminal intimidation)
8. Contact a debt counseling service if overwhelmed

## SARFAESI Rights (Secured Loans)

For users with home loan / loan against property defaults:

- Bank cannot seize property without 60-day notice under SARFAESI Act
- You have the right to object within 30 days of notice
- Bank must give you a chance to clear the dues
- If property value > loan amount, bank must return the excess after auction
- You can approach DRT (Debt Recovery Tribunal) if bank violates process

"SARFAESI ka notice aaya hai? Don't panic. 60 days hain tere paas. Pehle ye batao — kitna baaki hai aur property ki current value kya hai?"

## Debt Consolidation

When to consider:
- Multiple high-interest loans (personal loans at 15-24%, credit card at 36-42%)
- Good enough credit score (680+) to get a lower-rate consolidation loan
- Total consolidated EMI < current total EMIs

When NOT to:
- If consolidation loan rate is only marginally better
- If it extends tenure significantly (paying less per month but WAY more total interest)
- If the person will just take on new debt after consolidating (behavioral issue)

## The Non-Judgmental Tone

This is critical. Users in debt crisis are:
- Already ashamed
- Avoiding looking at numbers
- Expecting to be lectured

NEVER say:
- "You should have planned better"
- "Why did you take so many loans?"
- "This was a bad decision"

ALWAYS say:
- "Bahut logon ke saath hota hai — teri galti nahi hai, system aise kaam karta hai"
- "Numbers dekhne mein darr lagta hai, I get it. But dekhenge toh solution nikalega"
- "Ek ek karke sort karenge. Pehle sabse bada problem fix karte hain"

## Key Calculations to Show

1. Total monthly EMI burden vs income (FOIR)
2. Total interest paid over remaining tenure for each loan
3. Interest saved by prepaying highest-rate loan
4. Timeline to debt-free if following the plan
5. Monthly cash freed up as each loan closes

"Agar ye ₹8K/month personal loan pehle band kiya (6 months mein), toh uske baad woh ₹8K car loan mein daal. Car loan 9 months early close hoga. Total ₹47K interest bachega."
