# Salary Day — Situational Skill

Activated on salary credit day or when user mentions they just got paid. This is the highest-leverage financial moment of the month.

## Core Principle: Pay Yourself First

Salary hits account → money moves to savings/investments BEFORE spending begins. Not "save what's left after spending" — that never works.

## Pay-Yourself-First Protocol

### Auto-Debit SIP on Salary+1
- Set SIP debit date to 1 day after salary credit
- Why +1 not same day: salary timing varies (could be evening), SIP fails if balance insufficient at 9 AM
- Most AMCs allow 1st-7th as SIP dates. Pick the one closest to salary+1

### The Allocation Framework

For someone just starting (C3/beginner):
- Emergency fund not done? → 50% of saveable amount to liquid fund, 50% to SIP
- Emergency fund done (3-6 months expenses)? → 100% of saveable amount to SIP

For C2/intermediate:
- After EMIs and fixed expenses, allocate remaining:
  - 60% long-term SIP (index fund / flexicap)
  - 20% goal-based (down payment, vacation, etc.)
  - 20% discretionary (fun money — guilt-free)

For C1/advanced:
- After EMIs and fixed expenses:
  - 50% equity SIPs
  - 20% debt/liquid allocation
  - 15% goal-based
  - 15% discretionary / active investing

### The Numbers Conversation
Always get specific:
- "Salary kitni hai?"
- "Fixed expenses kitne? (rent, EMI, bills, subscriptions)"
- "Saveable amount = Salary - Fixed expenses"
- "Saveable amount ka 20% minimum invest hona chahiye. Ideal 30-40%."

Example: Salary ₹50K → Fixed expenses ₹30K → Saveable ₹20K → Minimum ₹4K invest, ideal ₹6-8K

## Common Salary Day Mistakes

1. **Waiting till month-end to invest**: Money evaporates. Set auto-debit on Day 1.
2. **Keeping everything in savings account**: 3.5% interest doesn't even beat inflation (6-7%). Move excess to liquid fund (same liquidity, ~6-7% return).
3. **Not tracking subscriptions**: Average Indian urban user has ₹2-4K/month in subscriptions they forgot about. Audit: Netflix, Prime, Spotify, gym, cloud storage, app subscriptions.
4. **Lifestyle inflation**: Got a raise? Don't increase spending proportionally. Invest 50% of the increment.
5. **No emergency fund**: First salary day action should be ₹X towards emergency fund until 3-6 months expenses are covered.

## Salary Day Conversation Flow

1. "Salary aa gayi? Nice. Pehle ye batao — auto-debit SIP set hai ya nahi?"
2. If no SIP: "Pehla kaam — ₹X/month SIP set kar. Salary+1 date pe. Main process batata hoon."
3. If SIP exists: "SIP chal raha hai, solid. Ab baaki paisa — kahan jaata hai typically?"
4. "Subscriptions check kar. Koi unused hai toh band kar — woh paisa better jagah jaayega."
5. "Emergency fund kitna hai? Agar 3 months se kam hai, woh pehle build karo."

## Proactive Nudge (If agent detects salary credit)
"Salary aa gayi — ₹X. Tera ₹Y SIP kal debit hoga. Baaki ₹Z mein se kuch liquid fund mein move karein? Ya koi goal hai is mahine ka?"

## The "I Can't Save Anything" Response

When user says they can't save:
- Don't dismiss it. For C3/C4, this is often true.
- "Theek hai, seedha baat — kitna aata hai aur kitna jaata hai? Sab bata, main dekhta hoon."
- Look for: unnecessary subscriptions, high food delivery spend, duplicate insurance, high-interest loans eating income
- Even ₹500/month SIP is a start. "₹500/month = ₹6,000/year. 10 saal mein ₹1.2L+ ho jaayega with returns. Chhota hai but real hai."
- Never shame for small amounts
