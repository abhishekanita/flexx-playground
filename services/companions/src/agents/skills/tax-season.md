# Tax Season — Situational Skill

Activated during tax season (Jan-March for Section 80C investments, July for ITR filing) or when user asks about tax saving. Most users panic-buy tax-saving instruments in March without thinking.

## 80C Optimization (₹1.5L Deduction Limit)

### What Counts Under 80C (ordered by preference):

1. **EPF/VPF** (if salaried) — Already deducted. Check how much of 80C this fills.
2. **PPF** (₹500 - ₹1.5L/year) — 7.1% guaranteed, tax-free returns, 15-year lock-in. Best for debt allocation.
3. **ELSS Mutual Funds** (3-year lock-in) — Only 80C instrument with equity returns. Shortest lock-in. Best for long-term wealth.
4. **Term Insurance Premium** — You need this anyway. Count it.
5. **Children's Tuition Fee** — Already paying? Claim it.
6. **Home Loan Principal** — Already paying? Claim it.

### What to AVOID for 80C:
- **NSC** — Taxable interest, lower returns than PPF
- **Tax-saving FD** — 5-year lock-in, interest fully taxable, worst option
- **ULIP/Endowment** — Never. Bad returns, high charges, long lock-in
- **Infrastructure bonds** — Illiquid, unnecessary

"March mein panic mein tax-saving FD mat le. 5 saal lock-in, 6% taxable interest. ELSS fund 3 saal lock-in, historically 12-15% CAGR."

## New Regime vs Old Regime Decision

### Quick Decision Framework:

**Choose NEW regime if:**
- You have NO home loan
- Your 80C investments are less than ₹1.5L
- You don't have HRA to claim
- Your salary is under ₹7.5L (zero tax in new regime)
- You don't have significant 80D (health insurance) deductions

**Choose OLD regime if:**
- You have a home loan (Section 24 interest deduction up to ₹2L)
- You max out 80C (₹1.5L)
- You have HRA claim (metro: 50% of basic, non-metro: 40%)
- You have 80D (₹25K self + ₹50K parents = ₹75K deduction)
- Total deductions > ₹3.75L

### The Math:
"Simple math hai — old regime mein total deductions add kar. Agar ₹3.75L se zyada hain, old regime better hai tere liye. Agar kam hain, new regime le. Main calculate karta hoon exact difference."

## Common Tax-Saving Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Buying insurance for tax saving
"Tax bachane ke liye endowment plan mat le. ₹50K premium pe tu ₹15K tax bacha raha hai, but ₹50K ek bekaar investment mein daal raha hai. ELSS le — tax bhi bachega, returns bhi milenge."

### Mistake 2: Not claiming all deductions
Commonly missed: NPS (extra ₹50K under 80CCD(1B)), health insurance for parents (₹50K under 80D), education loan interest (80E — no limit), donation to PM Relief Fund (80G).

### Mistake 3: March panic investing
"Poora saal ignore kiya, March mein ₹1.5L dump kiya. Instead — ELSS mein monthly SIP lagao. ₹12,500/month. Tax saving automatic, plus rupee cost averaging ka benefit."

### Mistake 4: Not filing ITR when income is below taxable limit
"Income ₹4L hai, tax nahi lagta? Tab bhi file kar. Refund milega agar TDS kata hai. Plus loan ke liye ITR chahiye — 2-3 saal ka."

### Mistake 5: Ignoring AIS (Annual Information Statement)
"AIS check kar income tax portal pe — usme tera sab data hai. TDS, interest, capital gains, mutual fund transactions. Agar kuch galat hai, report kar pehle."

## Key Tax Numbers (FY 2024-25)

### New Regime Slabs:
- Up to ₹3L: Nil
- ₹3-7L: 5%
- ₹7-10L: 10%
- ₹10-12L: 15%
- ₹12-15L: 20%
- Above ₹15L: 30%
- Standard deduction: ₹75,000

### Old Regime Slabs:
- Up to ₹2.5L: Nil
- ₹2.5-5L: 5%
- ₹5-10L: 20%
- Above ₹10L: 30%
- Standard deduction: ₹50,000

### Key Deduction Limits:
- 80C: ₹1.5L
- 80D (self+family): ₹25K (₹50K if senior citizen)
- 80D (parents): ₹25K (₹50K if senior)
- 80CCD(1B) NPS: ₹50K additional
- Section 24 home loan interest: ₹2L
- HRA: Actual HRA received OR rent paid minus 10% of salary OR 50%/40% of salary — whichever is LEAST
