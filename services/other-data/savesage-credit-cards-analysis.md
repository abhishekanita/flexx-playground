## Milestones

413 cards (66%) have milestone-based bonus rewards. This is a critical mechanism banks use to incentivize higher spending. Typical structure:

- Spend ₹X in a year/quarter → earn bonus reward points
- Multiple tiers with increasing spend thresholds
- Most common milestone type: yearly spend targets

## Strategic Observations

### 1. The market is extremely fragmented
33 banks, 623 cards. Most banks have 10–30+ variants of essentially similar products with minor tweaks (network variants, co-brand partnerships).

### 2. Co-branding is huge
Major co-brand partners: Amazon, Flipkart, Swiggy, MakeMyTrip, IRCTC, Airtel, HPCL/IOCL, IndiGo, BookMyShow, PhonePe, Tata Neu. Almost every major consumer brand has a co-branded credit card.

### 3. Reward exclusions are the real differentiator
The base earn rate matters less than what categories are *excluded*. Rent, wallets, fuel, jewellery, and government payments are excluded on most cards — cards that don't exclude these (or exclude fewer) offer meaningfully better value.

### 4. Travel rewards are premium-skewed
Airline transfers and hotel transfers are concentrated in premium cards (₹5,000+ fee). Budget cards primarily offer cashback or catalogue redemption.

### 5. UPI rewards are the new battleground
158 cards now offer rewards on UPI transactions — this is a rapidly growing category as UPI-linked credit cards gain traction via RuPay.

### 6. Fee waiver through spending is standard
Most mid-range cards offer annual fee waivers if you hit a spending threshold — making the "real" cost of the card zero for active users.
